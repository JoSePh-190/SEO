Who He Is

Gbarabe Tordi Joseph is a final-year Petroleum Engineering student at the University of Port Harcourt Nigeria's foremost institution for petroleum and energy engineering and a professional software developer with a specialisation in Python and the Django web framework.

He occupies a rare position in the Nigerian technical landscape: someone who has trained rigorously in the subsurface sciences and can simultaneously build the software to automate, communicate, and scale the work those sciences demand. Where most engineers reach for spreadsheets and most developers reach for generic business applications, Tordi builds purpose built tools, web applications that encode real engineering knowledge and put it directly in the hands of practitioners and students.

His undergraduate programme at UNIPORT has taken him through the full arc of hydrocarbon production: reservoir fluid behaviour, petrophysics, material balance analysis, enhanced oil recovery, well completion, production optimisation, and petroleum economics. His final-year research project applies computational methods to a live reservoir engineering problem — a fitting synthesis of the two disciplines he has spent years developing in parallel.

Flagship Project

MBE Calculator Solver

View Project →

The Material Balance Equation (MBE) is the foundational tool of reservoir engineering used to estimate original oil and gas in place and diagnose the dominant drive mechanism sustaining a reservoir's production. Gbarabe's solver accepts production history and fluid PVT data, performs numerical analysis, and returns graphical diagnostics (Havlena-Odeh straight-line plots and drive-index breakdowns) rendered directly in the browser. What once required a commercial simulator can now be completed in minutes by any engineer with an internet connection.

Python Django NumPy Matplotlib Reservoir Engineering
Applied EOR

EOR Calculator Solver

View Project →

Enhanced Oil Recovery screening requires evaluating a reservoir against established technical criteria across multiple recovery methods — water flooding, polymer flooding, CO₂ injection, steam injection, and in-situ combustion. Tordi's EOR Calculator automates this process: it evaluates a user-supplied reservoir description, ranks candidate EOR methods by suitability, and generates incremental recovery estimates producing in seconds the screening report that would otherwise take an engineer several hours to compile.

Python Django SciPy REST API EOR Screening
